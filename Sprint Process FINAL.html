Before Sprint Planning
Monday
(COP)
📅

Submit New Work via Workflow

  • Add to Dev Sprint Updates Slack channel using the workflow.
  • Include description, justification, estimate, dependencies.
  • Team review and decide if it enters the sprint or future.
  • You'll receive approval via the workflow.
  • If approved, move to "Selected for Development" in Monday.com.
Tuesday
(by 2PM)
📅

Bring Backlog Items Forward

  • Move suitable backlog items to "Selected for Development".
  • Ensure tickets have tags, descriptions, estimates and all key details.
  • Team review in the 2PM call and decide if it enters the sprint or future.
Wednesday
(AM)
📅

Review & Prepare

  • Review the new sprint and assigned work.
  • Collate any questions in advance.
  • Be prepared to raise them in the 10:00AM sprint planning call.

Key Changes
✓

Daily Stand-Ups are now 30 minutes

Focus on due dates, risks and support.

✓

Due Dates are now required for all tasks

Use realistic near-term (2–5 days) for urgent work, or end of sprint for non-urgent work.

✓

Tuesday Mid-Sprint Meeting removed

Replaced by enhanced daily stand-ups.

✓

Unplanned Work Tracking

All unplanned tasks must be raised via workflow, agreed by the team and tagged unplanned in Monday.com.

✓

Ticket Updates Required

Add updates in Monday.com (comment bubble) including progress, delays (internal/external) and early completions.

✓

NEW: Profile Picture Update

Ensure your Monday.com profile picture is up to date.

Ticket Management Standards
🗓️
Due Dates (Mandatory)
Every task must have a due date.
• Near-term for urgent work (e.g. 2-5 days)
• End of sprint for non-urgent work
💬
Ticket Updates (Mandatory)
Add updates in Monday.com (comment bubble). Include:
• Progress
• Delays (internal / external)
• Early completions
This helps with ownership and visibility across boards.
